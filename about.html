/ Origin

How I got here

I started at Esprit School of Engineering in Tunis in 2021 — not because I had a clear plan, but because I was curious about how systems think. The degree covered theory. The internships covered reality. I kept stacking both at the same time, which meant by the time I graduated in early 2025, I'd already shipped production AI in three countries.

The pattern that kept repeating: I'd join a project, realize the AI layer was either missing or held together with duct tape, and build the real thing. A RAG pipeline for parliamentary documents in Egypt. An AWS-deployed documentation assistant audited by NTT DATA in Munich. A QHSE AI assistant running on Spring AI and MCP in Sharjah. Each one taught me something the tutorial never mentioned.

Today I lead AI at 1morething Ventures out of Sharjah, teach bootcamp cohorts across three languages on the side, and work with clients from the Gulf to the US. The curiosity hasn't gone anywhere — it just ships faster now.

/ How I work

The approach

01
Ship first, learn in production
  • The real feedback loop only starts when a real user touches it. I bias hard toward getting something live, then iterating on the problems that actually show up — not the ones I imagined in a doc.
02
Build for the user, not the demo
  • A system that gets 95% accuracy in a controlled benchmark and 60% on real queries is a failed system. I care about what works when users ask things you didn't expect — edge cases aren't edge cases in production.
03
Teach while building
  • Running bootcamps in Arabic, French, and English while building production systems forces me to articulate what I actually know versus what I just got lucky with. It keeps the fundamentals sharp.
04
Curiosity compounds
  • The things I chased out of curiosity — Spring AI before it was mainstream, MCP when it was just a spec, Arabic NLP when nobody was writing about it — keep becoming the work. Staying ahead means staying curious.

"Ship first, learn in production. The feedback loop only starts when something real is in someone's hands."
